  • Apollo Urgent Care

    UI/UX Design

    • Project Overview

      Apollo Urgent Care Check-in app was part of a group project in my Human-Computer Interaction class. The instructions were to create a check-in form for an urgent care center that incoming patients would fill out upon arrival to get a doctor assigned to them. The objective was to create a check-in form that patients could fill in as quickly as possible, utilizing everything we had learned in class.

      My Contributions: As this was a group project/assignment to be completed in just 2 days, the primary objective was to have a prototype. My contributions mainly included deciding on the flow of screens, and deciding on how a patient can get assigned a doctor as quickly as possible. I also helped in designing the prototype.

    • Wireframe

      The wireframe depicts the user's journey and shows how the screens will look and the order. This provided the team with a guideline on how to design the patient check-in form.

    • Screen's

      The user flow for the Apollo Urgent Care Check-In app begins with Screen 1, a welcoming interface designed with a blue background to convey credibility, trust, knowledge, power, professionalism, cleanliness, calm, and focus. The screen instructs the patient to touch the screen to get started. Upon doing so, the user is directed to Screen 2, which asks if they have an emergency. If the patient selects 'Yes,' they are immediately taken to Screen 3, where a doctor is assigned to them without any further delay, ensuring no wait-time for emergency cases.


      If the patient selects 'No' on Screen 2, they are guided to Screen 4, which checks if they are a returning patient. Selecting 'Yes' here leads the patient to Screen 5, where they can schedule an appointment by entering their insurance number, insurance provider, and date of birth, or by scanning their insurance card for convenience. If the patient selects 'No' on Screen 4, indicating they are not a returning patient, they proceed to Screen 6.


      Screen 6 presents a check-in form for new patients to fill out, requesting all relevant data necessary for the appointment. After completing the form, the patient moves to Screen 7, where a doctor is assigned to them. The screen also displays the estimated wait time, providing the patient with a clear understanding of how long they will need to wait before seeing the doctor. This user flow ensures that all patients, whether new, returning, or in need of urgent care, are efficiently guided through the check-in process with minimal delay.
